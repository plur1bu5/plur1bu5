Hey, I'm Mohamed (aka plur1bu5). Penetration tester, red teamer, offensive security researcher, and cybersecurity engineering student at INPT. CPTS certified, HackTheBox Grandmaster. My main focus is Active Directory penetration testing, red teaming, and web application security, and I'm currently expanding into cloud, Kubernetes, and CI/CD pipeline security.

I recently got a [fix merged into Impacket](https://github.com/fortra/impacket/pull/2164) —raiseChild's golden ticket forging was broken on patched Windows Server 2022 because it stripped PAC buffers that modern DCs now validate, and silently ignored AES keys. That research led to [TrustFull](https://github.com/plur1bu5/TrustFull), a tool I built for AD trust enumeration and exploitation with multiple child-to-parent escalation techniques.

I've written working PoCs for [CVE-2025-2945](https://github.com/plur1bu5/CVE-2025-2945-pgadmin-rce) (RCE in pgAdmin 4 via eval() injection), [CVE-2024-51482](https://github.com/plur1bu5/CVE-2024-51482-PoC) (blind SQLi in ZoneMinder), and [CVE-2019-15949](https://github.com/plur1bu5/Nagios-CVE-2019-15949-RCE) (RCE in Nagios XI) when no working public exploits for me existed at the time.

Day to day I work with Impacket, BloodHound, Certipy, CrackMapExec/nxc, Burp Suite, and various C2 frameworks (Sliver, Havoc, Mythic). I also do DFIR and SOC work when needed, I've done Boss of the SOC competitions and Splunk hunting, and I like understanding both sides.

On the CTF side, I'm a core member of [Never7](https://ctftime.org/team/), one of Morocco's top teams, 1st at Cyber Warfare Warezone, 1st at AUSIM Cyber Drill, 2nd at Cyber Odyssey Finals, 3rd at NullHat Morocco, among others. I also compete internationally with 0xfun (ranked 2nd globally) where we placed 1st at KnightCTF 2026 and Pragyan CTF 2026. Previously part of QnQSec, where I helped organize our first international CTF (3000+ players), challenge design, infra, sponsorship, the whole thing.

I write about all of this at [plur1bu5.dev](https://plur1bu5.dev). 40+ writeups covering AD enterprise forest labs, HackTheBox machines, CTF challenges, and security research including [adversarial ML](https://plur1bu5.dev/posts/Adversarial-CNN-misclassification/) and [entropy analysis](https://plur1bu5.dev/posts/entropy-dual-perspective-analysis/). The [Impacket debugging writeup](https://plur1bu5.dev/posts/AD104-secdojo/) is probably the best starting point.
